# Things I Found Useful & Interesting

### 1. Visualization Theory and Best Practices (Storytelling, Minimalism, Tufte)
Introduction to the principles of effective chart design, avoiding chart junk, and storytelling with data.

* [Four Steps to Better Data Visualizations](https://www.centerforengagedlearning.org/four-steps-to-better-data-visualizations/) – Practical framework and key steps for creating more impactful data visualizations.
* [Simplify charts (Medium)](https://medium.com/counterarts/simplicity-is-a-data-visualization-best-friend-4ad4fbec30da) – An insightful read on why simplicity remains the ultimate goal and best friend of data visualization.
* [Storytelling & Design Chart (Medium)](https://gawarska-tywonek.medium.com/storytelling-a-simple-trick-to-grab-the-user-attention-8b89655e239c) – Straightforward design techniques and tricks to effectively capture and retain user attention.
* [Edward Tufte: Chartjunk](https://www.edwardtufte.com/bboard/q-and-a-fetch-msg?msg_id=00040Z) – A classic introduction to identifying and eliminating clutter from charts; excellent preparation before diving into layered graphics.
* [Add Two Digital Blog](https://www.addtwodigital.com/blog) – A collection of articles, insights, and inspirations focused strictly on data storytelling.
* [Autodesk: Datasaurus Dozen](https://www.autodesk.com/research/publications/same-stats-different-graphs) – A brilliant demonstration of why visualizing data is critical (shows multiple datasets with identical summary statistics but completely different visual distributions).

### 2. Foundations and the Theory of "Grammar of Graphics"
Theoretical materials explaining the underlying architecture and philosophy of modern data visualization systems.

* [Hadley Wickham: A Layered Grammar of Graphics (PDF)](http://byrneslab.net/classes/biol607/readings/wickham_layered-grammar.pdf) – The fundamental academic paper detailing the layered framework that powers modern R visualization.
* [Computing for Social Sciences: Why Visualize Data?](https://cfss.uchicago.edu/notes/why-visualize-data/) – Introduction to visualization theory, along with notes covering Grammar of Graphics, tidy data, text analysis, ML, and Shiny.
* [Computing for Social Sciences: Grammar of Graphics](https://cfss.uchicago.edu/notes/grammar-of-graphics/) – An accessible breakdown of the core components that make up the grammar of graphics.
* [Towards Data Science: A Comprehensive Guide to the Grammar of Graphics](https://towardsdatascience.com/a-comprehensive-guide-to-the-grammar-of-graphics-for-effective-visualization-of-multi-dimensional-1f92b4ed4149/) – An in-depth, structured guide to representing multi-dimensional data visually.
* [Grammar of Graphics Basics (QCBS)](http://r.qcbs.ca/workshop03/book-en/preparing-for-the-workshop.html) – Hands-on workshop materials designed to prepare learners for working with `ggplot2`.

### 3. The ggplot2 Ecosystem – Handbooks, Guides, and Examples (R)
Implementation materials ranging from initial setup and comparisons to advanced, production-ready code galleries.

* [Hadley Wickham: ggplot2. Elegant Graphics for Data Analysis (3rd Ed, online)](https://ggplot2-book.org/) – The complete, free online edition of the official handbook written by the creator of the package.
* [Claus O. Wilke: Fundamentals of Data Visualization](https://clauswilke.com/dataviz/) – An exceptional, highly recommended online textbook focusing on the principles of accurate and honest data presentation.
* [Variance Explained: Why I use ggplot2](http://varianceexplained.org/r/why-I-use-ggplot2/) – A direct comparison between R's base graphics and `ggplot2` (highly recommended reading!).
* [Variance Explained: Teaching ggplot2 to beginners](http://varianceexplained.org/r/teach_ggplot2_to_beginners/) – A pedagogical perspective on how to introduce this package to novices effectively.
* [STHDA: Be Awesome in ggplot2 (Practical Guide)](http://www.sthda.com/english/wiki/be-awesome-in-ggplot2-a-practical-guide-to-be-highly-effective-r-software-and-data-visualization) – **Excellent practical guide** for building highly polished, professional charts in R.
* [Datanovia: GGplot Examples Best Reference](https://www.datanovia.com/en/blog/ggplot-examples-best-reference/) – **Superb reference source** that covers standard plots alongside highly useful extensions and add-ons.
* [Top 50 Ggplot2 Visualizations Master List](http://r-statistics.co/Top50-Ggplot2-Visualizations-MasterList-R-Code.html) – A comprehensive repository of the 50 most common chart types, complete with reproducible R source code.
* [ggplot2 Quick Reference & Cheatsheet](http://r-statistics.co/ggplot2-cheatsheet.html) – A quick-access cheat sheet detailing fundamental functions, aesthetics, and parameters.
* [The R Graph Gallery: ggplot2 section](https://r-graph-gallery.com/ggplot2-package.html) – An interactive showcase of inspiration, design ideas, and ready-to-use code templates.
* [Cookbook for R: Graphs](http://www.cookbook-r.com/Graphs/) – A practical, recipe-based collection addressing common issues encountered when plotting data.
* [Cookbook for R: Correlation matrix](http://www.cookbook-r.com/Graphs/Correlation_matrix/) – A targeted tutorial focusing specifically on creating and styling correlation matrices.
* [R4Stats: Graphics with ggplot2](https://r4stats.com/examples/graphics-ggplot2/) – Clear examples contrasting different plotting paradigms with side-by-side code snippets.
* [Ben Shneiderman’s Visualization Mantra in ggplot2](https://jtr13.github.io/cc21/ben-shneidermans-visualization-mantra.html) – A practical implementation of the famous UI design mantra (*"Overview first, zoom and filter, then details-on-demand"*) using ggplot2.

### 4. Alternative Approaches in R: The Lattice Package
For comparative analysis – tools designed for generating trellis (conditional) multipanel displays for multivariate datasets.

* [Lattice Graphs (STHDA)](http://www.sthda.com/english/wiki/lattice-graphs) – A straightforward, clear introduction to generating plots within the `lattice` framework.
* [Tutorial on Lattice Package in R](https://jtr13.github.io/cc21fall2/tutorial-on-lattice-package-in-r.html) – A step-by-step user guide showcasing the primary capabilities of the library.
* [TechVidvan: Lattice Package in R](https://techvidvan.com/tutorials/lattice-package-in-r/) – A conceptual tutorial analyzing the internal structure and use cases of trellis graphics.
* [DataFlair: R Lattice Package Introduction](https://data-flair.training/blogs/r-lattice-package/) – Foundational theory and practical code examples for rendering multi-panel layouts.

### 5. R Workshops, Courses, and General Document Composition
Supplementary materials extending into general R programming mastery and academic document preparation.

* [YaRrrr! The Pirate's Guide to R](https://bookdown.org/ndphillips/YaRrr/) – An incredibly engaging, humorous, and comprehensive free textbook for learning R from absolute scratch.
* [Joey Stanley: R Workshops](https://joeystanley.com/pages/r-workshops) – A valuable repository of scripts, slides, and files from advanced data analysis workshops.
* [Joey Stanley: LaTeX Workshops](https://joeystanley.com/pages/latex-workshops) – Practical resources and introductory guides for typesetting scientific documents and technical layouts using LaTeX.
